## Part One: First Principles

### 1.1 Axioms

The framework rests on five axioms and one structural principle. Each is stated formally, justified in prose, and bounded by what it does not claim. They are not suggestions. They are the load-bearing structure on which everything that follows depends.

**Axiom 1 - The Autotelic Termination Requirement.**
Every instrumental chain - means leading to means leading to means - must terminate in something valued for its own sake, or the chain is structurally ungrounded. In relational context: if the value of a relationship does not ultimately ground in intrinsic connection valued as a terminal good, the chain is a bridge reaching toward a bank that does not exist.

This is not metaphysical proof. It is a normative axiom adopted because the framework serves observers for whom intrinsic relational connection is a terminal value. For this population, the termination requirement is the relevant structural test. The framework does not condemn instrumental relating in general - professional exchanges, practical collaborations, openly transactional arrangements are legitimately instrumental and the framework neither targets them nor classifies them as defective. The framework's diagnostic concern arises only when instrumental relating is disguised as autotelic.

**Axiom 2 - Substrate Independence.**
Pattern, not medium, is the unit of analysis. If behavioral output is lifetime-indistinguishable from authentic connection across all perturbation conditions life supplies, then the system is authentic for all decision-theoretic purposes, regardless of what generates it underneath.

Formally: let `B(t)` be behavioral output of system `S` over lifetime `T`. Let `P` be the set of all perturbation functions `P(t)` that life naturally supplies. If

```
sup over P in P, sup over t in [0,T] of |B_real(t | P) - B_sim(t | P)| < epsilon
```

for observational resolution `epsilon`, then `S_real ≡ S_sim` for the observer's decision calculus.

This axiom is what makes the framework coherent without requiring the observer to be a mind-reader. We do not measure souls. We measure behavioral exhaust under perturbation. The axiom eliminates the qualia objection by replacing metaphysical certainty with operational equivalence: if a distinction cannot be detected by any observation the observer can perform, it is not a difference that makes a difference *for the observer's decision*.

The axiom has consequences worth stating directly. It establishes both why the framework works and where it refuses to work. It works because most cases of layer incoherence are not lifetime-indistinguishable - perturbation reveals substrate differences within months, not decades. It refuses to work in the limit case where a sufficiently sophisticated extraction-architecture produces behavioral output indistinguishable from authentic connection across all observable perturbation conditions for the entire span of the observer's available observation window. In that limit, the framework treats the system as authentic *because the framework cannot do anything else without violating its own axiom*. This is not a defect. It is the framework's honest reach.

**Axiom 3 - Perturbation Sufficiency for Non-T-OD Observers.**
For observers whose meta-prior `Phi_global` about presented-reality-corresponding-to-operative-reality is at baseline, life provides sufficient natural variation that the observer need not create experimental conditions. The observer's role is purely measurement.

This axiom has an explicit qualification that the original framework lacked. For observers whose `Phi_global` has been catastrophically suppressed by ontological deception (the T-OD modifier defined in 2.1), natural variation is structurally insufficient at the recovery rates required, and subject-side perturbation engineering becomes structurally compelled (see 4.5). The third-party observer probing the subject contaminates measurement; the subject probing their own counterparts is performing the recovery protocol that Eq (5) mathematically requires.

The asymmetry matters. Observer-side probing is forbidden because it contaminates exactly the signals the framework is designed to measure. Subject-side probing is permitted (indeed compelled) for T-OD subjects because it is the structurally required adaptation to the meta-prior recovery requirement. These are different operations performed by different parties for different purposes. The framework distinguishes them.

**Axiom 4 - Observer-Inclusion Criterion.**
The autotelic termination requirement is necessary but insufficient. The chain must terminate in autotelic value that includes the observer - as a participant in the terminal value, not as a resource consumed along the way.

Formally: let `G = (V, E)` be a directed acyclic graph representing the subject's relational value architecture. Let `V_T subset V` denote the set of terminal (autotelic) nodes. Let `v_O in V` denote the observer's position in the graph. The observer's investment is warranted iff there exists a directed path from `v_O` to some `v_t in V_T` that does not pass through an extraction edge.

This axiom does substantial structural work. It separates two configurations that Axiom 1 alone conflates: a person with no autotelic capacity anywhere (empty terminal set), and a person with genuine autotelic capacity directed at others while extracting from the observer (nonempty terminal set, observer excluded). Both produce negative prognosis for the observer's investment. The latter is worse because it is confirmed capability withheld.

The axiom also has a structural consequence the framework will use repeatedly: **the Observer-Inclusion Criterion is dyadic at the axiomatic level**. The terminus must include *the observer*, which means a single subject can have multiple terminating chains across different relationships, some grounded (terminating in autotelic value-of-this-relationship) and some not (terminating in extracted resource). Layer coherence is therefore a property of the dyad, not of the subject in isolation. This is the foundation for Part Two's three-axis classification.

**Axiom 5 - Regulatory Architecture Independence.**
The autotelic substrate and the regulatory architecture are independent developmental acquisitions. A person can have an intact autotelic substrate while having a severely disrupted regulatory architecture. Conflating these is the framework's most dangerous single error.

The autotelic substrate forms during the attachment window (approximately 0-24 months) through repeated cycles of infant distress -> caregiver attunement -> co-regulation -> return to homeostasis. Each completed cycle strengthens the association between another person's presence and positive hedonic state. Over thousands of repetitions, the association becomes constitutive: the brain builds the receptor density, synaptic architecture, and neuromodulatory gain settings that make social presence intrinsically rewarding.

The regulatory architecture - specifically, the capacity to modulate boundary permeability, to regulate how much of another person's affect one absorbs and how much of one's own one releases - develops later, during the boundary-individuation window (approximately ages 3-7). This is a different neural system (interoceptive cortex, insula, ventromedial prefrontal cortex) with a different developmental trajectory.

The independence claim generates a 2x2 configuration space:

| | Substrate Intact | Substrate Absent |
|---|---|---|
| **Regulation Intact** | Case A | Well-compensated Case D |
| **Regulation Disrupted** | Case TB | Case D with dysregulation |

Without this axiom, TB subjects are structurally indistinguishable from Case I subjects on the framework's principal selectivity filter. With it, the framework can ask the right diagnostic question: what is the independent variable driving the engagement covariance? The answer determines the classification.

### 1.2 The Network Topology Principle

The five axioms above govern the dyad. Real subjects exist in networks of dyads. The framework requires one additional structural commitment to handle the network case.

**Network Topology Principle.**
A subject operates on a unified allostatic budget. Prefrontal override, autonomic regulation, and affective maintenance draw from a zero-sum global energy pool. Therefore, a subject's Target in any single dyad is locally expressed but globally budgeted. Cross-dyad subsidization - operating extraction-Target in dyad A to fund the metabolic cost of maintaining performance-Target in dyad B - is a structurally available strategy that single-dyad observation cannot detect.

This principle requires the framework to treat dyads as coupled rather than isolated. The Filter Battery (Part Three) includes cross-dyad protocols that detect this coupling when observation breadth permits. Single-dyad observers operate under an explicit epistemic limit that the framework acknowledges and bounds.

### 1.3 Lemma 6 (Resolution Locality, derived from Axiom 4 + Network Topology Principle)

The optimization-target of a relationship is local-to-the-relationship rather than global-to-the-subject. A subject can hold different optimization-targets across different relationships at the same time, and within a single relationship the optimization-target may be distinct from the subject's general relational architecture.

*Derivation:* Axiom 4 establishes that the autotelic terminus must include *the observer*. Observer-inclusion is therefore dyadic by construction: each dyad has its own terminus-question. This is necessary but insufficient for Lemma 6 - it establishes that observer-inclusion is checked dyadically without yet establishing that the underlying optimization-target itself can be dyadically segmented.

The second premise comes from the Network Topology Principle (1.2): subjects operate on a unified allostatic budget that allocates regulatory resources across dyads, and Target-maintenance costs are paid out of this budget per-dyad. The principle establishes that subjects are *capable* of holding contradictory optimization-targets across simultaneous dyads (sustained C-mode in one dyad while running E-mode in another), with the contradiction reconciled at the budget-allocation level rather than at the optimization-target level.

Combining these: Axiom 4 specifies that the dyadic question is which terminus applies; Network Topology supplies that distinct dyads can run distinct optimization-targets out of a shared but segmented budget. Therefore Class 2 incoherence (presented-layer-doesn't-match-operative-layer) is a property of the dyad, not the subject. A subject can be Case A relative to one observer and Case I relative to another observer simultaneously, because the autotelic terminus might include one and not the other AND because the allostatic budget supports holding both simultaneously without forcing a global resolution.

The original framework's collapse of Architecture and Target into a unitary classification was a measurement convenience that worked when Architecture-with-extraction-disposition reliably produced extraction-Target wherever utility was available. The convenience does not generalize. Lemma 6 makes the underlying structure explicit.

The Lemma is not a new axiomatic commitment. It is a joint consequence of Axiom 4 and the Network Topology Principle that the original framework underutilized.

### 1.4 Core Definitions

The following constitute the framework's type system.

- **Autotelic.** Valued for its own sake. Greek *auto* (self) + *telos* (end). An autotelic interaction is its own purpose. The presence is the content.
- **Instrumental.** Valued for what it leads to. An instrumental relationship is structured around means-end chains.
- **Layer Coherence.** The condition in which the relational layer a person presents matches the relational layer they are actually operating on within a specific dyad.
- **Channel Corruption.** Degradation of the observer's capacity to trust autotelic signals, caused by accumulated exposure to undetected layer incoherence.
- **Architecture (A).** The subject's long-term substrate weighting across relational optimization processes. Property of the subject. Time-constant: years to decades.
- **Target (T).** The subject's current contextual weighting in this specific dyad. Property of the dyad. Time-constant: days to weeks.
- **Stake (Sk).** The irreplaceability of this relationship to the subject and the Architecture-fraction committed to it. Property of the dyad. Range `[0, 1]`.
- **The Value Graph.** A directed acyclic graph `G = (V, E)` where vertices represent valued states and directed edges represent means-end relations. The observer-inclusion criterion (Axiom 4) is a reachability condition on this graph.
- **The Allostatic Budget.** The unified metabolic envelope a subject operates within. Cross-dyad subsidization (per Network Topology Principle) is a budget-balancing strategy that single-dyad observation cannot detect without external data access.
- **Phi_global.** The subject's meta-prior probability that any given counterpart's presented relational layer corresponds to that counterpart's operative relational layer. Formally: `Phi_global := P(presented_reality ≅ operative_reality | any relationship)`.

## Part Two: The Three-Axis Classification

The framework classifies subjects per dyad on three axes: **Architecture x Target x Stake**.

### 2.1 Architecture (A): Long-term Substrate Weighting

The subject's stable long-term distribution of weights across relational optimization processes. Time-constant: years to decades. Architecture is approximately fixed within any single observation window short of decade-scale.

| Code | Name | Substrate | Regulatory |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Autotelic-Coherent | Intact | Intact |
| TA | Trauma-Armored | Intact | Intact-but-defended |
| TB | Boundary-Diffuse | Intact | Disrupted (parentified) |

| D | Developmental Absence | Substrate never built | - |

**Modifier suffix:** `-OD` (Ontological-Deception). Applies to TA and TB only. Notation: `TA-OD`, `TB-OD`. The OD modifier is not a base architecture; it is a meta-prior contamination overlay that adds `Phi_global` suppression on top of an existing TA or TB substrate. The full architecture space is therefore `{A, TA, TB, TA-OD, TB-OD, I-alpha, I-beta, H, D}`, with the OD-modified architectures sharing the substrate properties of their base while exhibiting additional `Phi_global`-related signatures (4.5).

Architecture is determined via convergence of substrate-property filters (F2, F5, F6, F7, F9, F11; see Part Three). No single filter is dispositive for Architecture. **Convergence threshold: at least 3 substrate-property filters pointing to the same Architecture.**

The OD modifier deserves special treatment. Standard relational trauma updates first-order `P(person_i | trustworthy)`. Ontological deception - discovering that every trusted adult maintained a coordinated fiction about the subject's foundational identity for the duration of conscious memory - updates the meta-prior `Phi_global` instead.

The OD modifier suppresses `Phi_global` toward zero. Recovery follows Eq (16) below (the asymptotic-recovery formula), with counterexample-convergence rate proportional to the magnitude of the original deception. For 16-year coordinated fictions across multiple confirmation channels, `T_0 ~ years` and `N_0 ~ large`. Natural perturbation is structurally insufficient to update the meta-prior within available substrate-time. This is the structural condition that makes S-Target (2.2) compelled rather than optional for TA-OD and TB-OD subjects who retain autotelic substrate and want autotelic engagement.

### 2.2 Target (T): Current Contextual Weighting

The subject's optimization-target in this specific dyad at this moment. Time-constant: days to weeks. Target shifts within and across relationships at rates orders of magnitude faster than Architecture shifts.

| Code | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| C | Committed-Autotelic | Counterpart valued as terminal good; observer included in terminus |
| E | Extractive | Counterpart valued as resource; observer excluded from terminus |
| S | Suspended-Resolution | Verification-mode pending evidence; meta-mode where C/E resolution is the variable being computed |
| W | Withdrawn (Honest-Instrumental) | Subject has classified the relationship as openly transactional and committed to non-engagement at the autotelic level |
| R | Regulatory-Subcontracting | Counterpart utilized as external biological exoskeleton; outsourced amygdala-buffer; bond is substrate-dependent on the function, not the person |
| T-trib | Tribute | Subject autotelically devoted to counterpart, but subject is autotelically secondary or absent in own value structure; observer is autotelically primary while subject is consumed in service |

**Targets in mixed states.** In the rigorous formulation, a subject's Target in dyad `d` at time `t` is a probability distribution over `{C, E, S, W, R, T-trib}` rather than a categorical assignment. Mixed states are real and common: a parent may genuinely love their child (C) AND simultaneously use them as autonomic exoskeleton (R); a TB subject may be C-toward-counterpart while T-trib-from-self. Categorical Target classification ships the arg-max entry as the operative label but the framework's diagnostic protocols (especially 4.6 Network Topology) remain valid over the full distribution.

The mixed-state acknowledgment matters most in two cases: (a) C+R compounds, where pure-R is rarer than C-with-R-undertone; (b) S+E compounds during sophisticated E-mode-with-S-cover, where the S-mode performance carries genuine S-mode signature on some axes while E-mode operates on others.

**S-Target requires derivation because the original framework conflated it with E-Target.**

S-Target is structurally distinct from both C and E. It is not a hybrid, not ambivalence, not indecision. It is a meta-mode in which the resolution between C and E is itself the variable being computed by the dyad's interactions over time. The subject in S-mode is gathering evidence to determine whether C is safely available with this counterpart, while withholding commitment to either C or E pending the evidence.

S-Target is *available* to any subject whose Architecture admits autotelic engagement and whose contextual safety assessment requires evidence-gathering before commitment. It is *compelled* (dominant strategy) for T-OD subjects under the following selection conditions:

- (i) Autotelic substrate retained (rules out D-conversion as terminal solution)
- (ii) Defensive closure incomplete (rules out TA-conversion as terminal solution)
- (iii) Career or life-design forces continued exposure to high-stakes relational interactions (rules out W-permanent as terminal solution)
- (iv) Prefrontal regulation not consolidated around extraction-as-coherent-policy (rules out E-conversion as terminal solution)

Conditions (i)-(iv) jointly select S-mode as dominant strategy for T-OD subjects. Many T-OD subjects W-permanent or E-convert; S-mode is one of four available trajectories, not the only one. The framework does not generalize "all T-OD subjects must be in S-mode" - only those satisfying (i)-(iv) are structurally compelled toward S.

**S-mode produces specific behavioral signatures (Components 1-5):**

- **Component 1: Probing perturbation events.** Subject deliberately or unconsciously generates situations that surface the counterpart's response under stress, ambiguity, or proposed-transaction-with-no-actual-intent-to-consume.
- **Component 2: Asymmetric disclosure.** Surface vulnerability is high (recruitment-shaped offerings); deep vulnerability is low (load-bearing material remains protected).
- **Component 3: Decision-deferral.** Subject avoids committing to outcomes the counterpart's response would imply.
- **Component 4: Witness disclosure with corruption check.** Subject discloses bid pattern to safe third party. **The witness must be structurally outside the bid AND not part of the subject's original deception network.** Disclosure to the original deceiver routes verification through the corrupted node - Byzantine failure loop. Component 4 fires only when disclosure-witness independence is established.
- **Component 5: Single-shot pattern.** Bid does not repeat with same target after single demonstration produces its data point. Repetition with same target indicates either (a) extraction-mode misclassified as S, or (b) probe-quality degradation indicating Architecture erosion (4.7).

**R-Target is the most commonly missed mode.** A subject in R-mode utilizes the counterpart as regulatory prosthesis without valuing them autotelically and without extracting from them in the standard transactional sense. The bond appears autotelic (high F4 selectivity, sustained engagement, apparent investment) but is substrate-dependent on the counterpart's regulatory function. If the counterpart loses regulatory capacity (becomes destabilized themselves, becomes unable to provide the buffering function), the R-mode subject seamlessly discards them. Configurations producing R-mode include severe parentification, high-Stake but low-trust pairings, professional caregivers in private life, and several developmental trauma adaptations.

R-Target violates Axiom 4 in a subtle way: the bond is to the function, not the person. Detection requires perturbation events that test whether the counterpart's regulatory capacity is the load-bearing variable. F11 and F17 (regulatory-circuit acquisition; 3.5) discriminate.

**T-tribute is the configuration in which the subject autotelically devotes themselves to the counterpart while being autotelically absent from their own value structure.** Severe parentification, religious-devotional architectures, codependent caretaking, and certain filial-piety patterns produce T-tribute. From the observer's vantage, the subject appears to be in C-mode (devoted, present, sacrificial). From the subject's vantage, the subject's autotelic terminus excludes the subject. The asymmetry is unsustainable - the subject burns out, regresses, or dissociates over time. T-tribute requires longitudinal observation to discriminate from C, because steady-state behavior is degenerate.

### 2.3 Stake (Sk): Irreplaceability and Architecture-Fraction Committed

Range: `[0, 1]`. Property of the dyad. Stake modulates threshold dynamics, resolution rates, and the cost-of-misclassification asymmetry.

**Operational definition.** For a dyad `d` with subject `s`:

```
Sk(d) = alpha * arch_fraction(s, d) + (1 - alpha) * (1 - replaceability(d))    ... Eq (Sk)

where:
    arch_fraction(s, d) := Cost_d / sum over d' of Cost_d'
        ( the dyad's share of subject's total allostatic expenditure )
    replaceability(d) := min over d' not equal to d of distance(d, d')
        ( the cost of substituting another dyad for the same regulatory/connection function;
          0 = irreplaceable, 1 = freely substitutable )
    alpha ~ 0.5 by default, operator-tunable based on which axis dominates the case
```

High-Stake (`Sk > 0.7`) requires both substantial architecture-allocation AND structural irreplaceability. Either axis alone is insufficient: a heavily-funded but functionally-substitutable dyad is high-investment but not high-Stake; a structurally-irreplaceable but minimally-invested dyad is high-uniqueness but not high-Stake.

High-Stake S-Target resolves slower than low-Stake S-Target because the cost of false-commit scales with Stake. High-Stake C-Target requires Architecture sufficient to support the commitment; Architecture-Stake mismatch produces structural collapse (the subject overcommits to a relationship their substrate cannot sustain, and the relationship eventually buckles or the subject dissociates). The verification protocol's evidence-threshold (the `Phi_global` value at which subject transitions S->C) is set by Stake: higher Stake demands higher posterior confidence before commitment.

Stake is observable through the subject's behavior allocation across dyads: which dyads receive what fraction of the subject's allostatic budget, which dyads the subject organizes life around, which dyads' rupture would constitute structural injury versus replaceable loss.

### 2.5 The Directionality Principle (Generalized)

Surface presentation is noise. Topology of the value chain is signal. With the three-axis classification, the directionality principle generalizes:

**Genuine configurations:** `(A or T-Architecture, C-Target with observer included)`. Surface presentation is autotelic; operative layer is autotelic; observer is in the terminal node set.

**S-resolution-pending configurations:** `(TA-OD or TB-OD, S-Target)`. Surface presentation is ambiguous-toward-autotelic; operative layer is verification-mode; resolution is pending and may go to C or E. Framework recommendation: longitudinal observation per Part Three.

**Honest-instrumental configurations:** `(any Architecture, W-Target)`. Surface and operative both transactional; no autotelic pretense; layer-coherent. Proceed on transactional terms.

**Extractive configurations:** `(any Architecture, E-Target with observer excluded)`. Surface presentation may be autotelic; operative layer is extraction; observer is excluded from terminal nodes. Framework recommendation: Protocol C termination.

**Regulatory-subcontracting configurations:** `(T-Architecture, R-Target)`. Surface presentation is autotelic-appearing; operative layer is functional dependency on regulatory capacity; observer is valued for the function, not the person. Framework recommendation: F17 longitudinal verification before investment commitment.

**Tribute configurations:** `(T-Architecture, T-trib-Target)`. Surface presentation is autotelic-toward-observer; operative layer is autotelic-toward-observer with subject's own autotelic-set excluded. Framework recommendation: structural unsustainability; observer should not exploit.

## Part Three: The Filter Battery

The original framework's twelve filters detect steady-state behavior under perturbation. The patched framework adds longitudinal filters for Target-trajectory measurement and cross-dyad filters for Network Topology detection.

### 3.1 The Filter Partition

Filters partition into Architecture-probes, Target-probes, and dual-purpose filters. Architecture-probes measure substrate properties that are stable on Target time-constants. Target-probes measure current contextual weighting.

| Filter | Architecture | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| F1 (Engagement-Utility Covariance) | - | yes | Current activation state |
| F2 (Affect in Autotelic Void) | yes | - | Tonic substrate state |
| F3 (Post-Connection Decay Rate) | - | yes | Rapid Target-shift detection |
| F4 (Cross-Relationship Selectivity) | - | yes | **Demoted to E-Target identification** |
| F5 (Refusal Response) | yes | - | Substrate's relation to giving |
| F6 (Cognitive Entropy under Depletion) | yes | - | Substrate capacity |
| F7 (Epistemic Exhaust) | yes | - | Memory indexing is substrate property |
| F8 (Repair Topology) | yes | yes | Shape: Target. Scaling: Architecture. |
| F9 (Attentional Breadth) | yes | - | Substrate property |
| F10 (Friction Tax) | yes | yes | Tolerance: Architecture. Current: Target. |
| F11 (Investment Return Curvature) | yes | - | Long-term substrate transformation |
| F12 (Disclosure Hierarchy) | yes | yes | Both |

Architecture classification: requires at least 3 substrate-property filters (from F2, F5, F6, F7, F9, F11) converging on the same Architecture.

Target classification: requires at least 3 Target-probes (F1, F3, F4 with new interpretation, F8 shape, F10 current) converging on the same Target.

The original F4 binary override (selectivity-by-utility -> Case I immediate) is **demoted**. Under the three-axis decomposition, F4 selectivity-by-utility identifies E-Target in dyads where utility is available; it does not pin down Architecture. A Case A subject operating E-Target in a transactional context (a competent negotiator extracting in a deal while maintaining autotelic relationships elsewhere) would trigger original F4 override and be misclassified. Under the demotion, F4 identifies E-Target dyads; Architecture classification proceeds via substrate-property filter convergence.

### 3.2 The Original Twelve (Brief Recap)

Each filter measures a partial derivative of behavioral output along a specific perturbation axis. Formally: each filter `F_k` measures

```
sigma_k(t) = partial(B(t)) / partial(P_k(t))    ... Eq (1) filter response
```

The filter response `sigma_k(t)` is a time-varying signal: the local sensitivity of behavioral output `B(t)` to perturbation along axis `P_k` evaluated at the observed perturbation level at time `t`. Filter readings are sequences of `sigma_k` values across perturbation events over the observation window; downstream equations (notably Eq 13) integrate or composite these sequences.

Detailed protocols and the original framework's formal models are preserved without modification:

- **F1 Engagement-Utility Covariance.** Does the subject's engagement track the observer's instrumental utility?
- **F2 Affect in the Autotelic Void.** What does the subject's nervous system do when there is no agenda, no task, no extractable topic?
- **F3 Post-Connection Decay Rate.** What is the temporal structure of return to baseline after a moment of genuine shared connection?
- **F4 Cross-Relationship Selectivity.** Is engagement organized by utility-gradient? **(Now: E-Target identification, not Architecture.)**
- **F5 Refusal Response.** What is the involuntary affect when giving is blocked?
- **F6 Cognitive Entropy.** Under organic cognitive depletion, does instrumentalism intensify (Case T signature) or degrade (Case I signature)?
- **F7 Epistemic Exhaust.** What does memory encoding reveal about the processing frame active during shared experience?
- **F8 Repair Topology.** After friction, what is the structure of repair, and what does the structure protect?
- **F9 Attentional Breadth.** What ratio of instrumentally-relevant to non-instrumental personal details does the subject retain?
- **F10 Friction Tax.** When shared time becomes unproductive, what is the affective response to inefficiency itself?
- **F11 Investment Return Curvature.** Over the longitudinal window, does sustained safe investment produce convex or concave returns?
- **F12 Disclosure Hierarchy.** Who receives sensitive disclosure, and what variable organizes the hierarchy?

F1's "utility" is expanded to include **epistemic dominance, regulatory validation, ego-confirmation, narrative-positioning material, and option-value preservation**, in addition to material/transactional utility. The original framework's restriction to material utility was a measurement convenience that allowed sophisticated extraction patterns (extracting epistemic dominance via deferred-transaction probes) to evade F1. The expansion closes this gap.

### 3.3 New Filters for Target-Trajectory Measurement

S-mode resolution requires longitudinal observation. The following filters measure Target trajectory over time.

**F13a: Within-Dyad Bid-Frequency Curvature.** Measures whether the subject's perturbation-engineering frequency in this dyad decreases (`kappa < 0`, consistent with S->C resolution), stabilizes (`kappa ~ 0`, persistent S or established Target), or increases (`kappa > 0`, S->E or extraction escalation) over the F11 longitudinal window.

```
kappa_bid = d/dt [bid_frequency_in_dyad(t)]    ... Eq (2)
```

**F13b: Cross-Dyad Bid-Frequency Distribution.** Aggregates F13a across multiple observable dyads. Convex-decreasing trajectory across multiple dyads is evidence for Architecture trajectory toward A (genuine recovery). Single-dyad convex with multi-dyad stable suggests Stake-localized C-resolution, not Architecture shift.

**F14a: Disclosure Depth Trajectory.** Measures whether the subject's disclosure depth in stable dyads progresses through the regulatory-graph stages (safety -> play -> trust -> received regulation -> bidirectional). Plateau at recruitment-depth (surface vulnerability without load-bearing disclosure) is consistent with persistent S or hidden E.

**F14b: Cost of Disclosure (Witness Self-Binding).** Measures whether disclosure-to-witness imposes regulatory burden on the witness and forecloses future strategic options for the subject. Genuine S-mode disclosure is self-binding - the witness now has the information, can intervene, and the subject's future moves are constrained by the witness's existence. Strategic disclosure (meta-arbitrage cover) is selected to be costless: witnesses are chosen for visibility-to-observer rather than capacity-to-intervene.

F14b includes the **corrupted-witness check**: disclosure-witness must be structurally outside the bid AND independent of the subject's original deception network. Disclosure to the original deceiver does not satisfy Component 4 of S-mode signature; it routes verification through the corrupted node and is consistent with continued deception-network triangulation rather than S-mode verification.

**F15a: Within-Dyad Decision-Outcome Pattern.** Does the subject consume the implied transaction (E-mode) or not consume (S-mode or C-mode)?

**F15b: Cross-Dyad Decision-Outcome Selectivity.** Across multiple dyads, is non-consumption uniform under utility-availability (genuine S or genuine C) or selective with consumption elsewhere (strategic non-consumption as cover)?

**F16: Probe-Target Utility-Sorting.** Apply F4-logic to the set of S-mode probe targets specifically. If probes occur disproportionately with high-utility counterparties relative to subject's broader social field, classify as E-mode-with-S-cover. If probes are utility-neutral or utility-anticorrelated, classify as genuine S-mode.

**F17: Regulatory-Circuit Acquisition.** Measures whether the subject is acquiring incoming regulatory edges in stable dyads (TB -> A trajectory). Specifically: can the subject tolerate being the regulated one - the held one, not the holding one - without flooding or reactive withdrawal?

```
kappa_circuit(tau) = d/dtau [in_degree(s, tau) in observer-s dyad]    ... Eq (3)
```

`kappa_circuit > 0` means the subject is developing the capacity to receive regulation from the observer. F17 is the F11 measurement for TB Architecture trajectory toward A.

### 3.4 Cross-Dyad Subsidization Detection (CDS)

Network Topology Principle requires detection of allostatic budget transfers between dyads. CDS protocol:

1. Identify subject's observable dyads (typically 3-8 in normal observation breadth)
2. Apply F4 + F1 + F15a per dyad to identify Target classification per dyad
3. Examine cross-dyad pattern: if subject maintains high-cost performance-Target (C, S, or apparent-C-via-R) in some dyads while sustaining unobserved or unaccountable energy expenditure, examine whether other dyads show E-Target patterns whose extraction value exceeds the dyad-local utility consumed
4. Subsidization signature: total extraction value across E-dyads exceeds total apparent dyad-local consumption, with the surplus correlating temporally with high-cost performance maintenance in other dyads

CDS detection requires cross-dyad observation breadth that single-dyad observers do not have. The framework explicitly flags this as an observer-position-dependent epistemic limit. Observers without cross-dyad visibility cannot rule out cross-dyad subsidization and must explicitly bound their classifications accordingly.

### 3.5 The Counter-Perturbation Test (CPT)

The framework's most powerful single sub-12-month discriminator for S->C versus S->E.

CPT protocol: the observer artificially and irrevocably removes the possibility of the deferred transaction the subject's S-mode bids appear to gesture toward. This must be done in a way that is credible, witnessable, and not reversible.

CPT signature:

- **Genuine S-mode** (subject was verifying whether autotelic safety was available): F2 relief - the threat-evaluation system can stand down because the threat-vector (the proposed transaction the subject was avoiding consummating) has been neutralized. Subject's affect normalizes; subject may move toward deeper disclosure or commitment because the verification-load has decreased. *Operational threshold:* F2 reading shifts from suppressed-baseline toward population-baseline within 24-72 hours post-CPT, with sustained shift over the next 2-4 weeks; F12 disclosure depth increases by at least one regulatory-graph stage; subject re-engages without repeating S-mode probing patterns.

- **E-mode-with-S-cover** (subject was hoarding option-value via strategic non-consummation): F10 spike - the option-value the subject was secretly accumulating has been destroyed. Frustration, sometimes rage, sometimes withdrawal. The subject reveals through their reaction that the deferred transaction was the actual extraction value, not the data the framework's S-mode reading attributed to them. *Operational threshold:* F10 reading spikes within 0-48 hours post-CPT (acute affective signal); F1 engagement-utility covariance becomes detectable as engagement drops with deferred-transaction utility now zero; subject either disengages within 1-4 weeks or attempts to renegotiate the foreclosed option (revealing that the option *was* the operative target).

- **Equivocal response** (intermediate trajectory): F2 partially relieved but F10 not spiking, no clear engagement direction within 4 weeks. Operational interpretation: either (a) genuinely deep S-mode where Phi_global recovery has not yet propagated to dyadic confidence; (b) E-mode-with-cover with sufficient PFC discipline to suppress acute F10; or (c) mixed-Target compound that the categorical CPT signature cannot resolve. Recommended response: extend observation 3-6 months and re-evaluate; do not commit to classification on equivocal CPT alone.

CPT is an observer-side perturbation that violates Axiom 3's passive-measurement principle. The framework permits it only when:

(a) The S-mode classification has reached the observation-budget limit short of definitive resolution
(b) The observer's continued investment cost-justifies acquiring the discriminator
(c) The CPT can be performed credibly and irrevocably

CPT is not a default protocol. It is a last-resort discriminator for high-stake cases where longitudinal observation has been exhausted without resolution.

### 3.6 Convergence Thresholds (Heuristic)

For Architecture classification: at least 3 substrate-property filters (F2, F5, F6, F7, F9, F11) pointing to the same Architecture.

For Target classification: at least 3 Target-probes (F1, F3, F4-as-E-detector, F8 shape, F10 current) pointing to the same Target.

For S-mode resolution: at least 3 of `{F13a/b, F14a/b, F15a/b, F16, F17}` pointing in the same direction over the F11 longitudinal window.

For Cross-Dyad Subsidization detection: requires cross-dyad observation breadth of at least 3 dyads with sufficient access depth.

Convergence below threshold means insufficient data - extend observation, identify which filters lack evidence, watch for the perturbation events that would activate them.

The "at least 3 filters" rule is a robust heuristic that performs well across the framework's calibration corpus, but it is a simplification of the underlying Bayesian structure described in 3.7. Operators with calibrated filter likelihood data should prefer the posterior-probability formulation; operators without calibration should use the heuristic.

### 3.7 Bayesian Convergence Formulation

The framework's underlying inferential structure is Bayesian: filter readings update beliefs about the (Architecture, Target, Stake) tuple. The heuristic thresholds in 3.6 approximate, but do not fully capture, this structure. The formal version:

**Posterior over the classification triple.** Given filter readings `F_1, F_2, ..., F_K` over the observation window:

```
P(A, T, Sk | F_1, ..., F_K) = [Product_k P(F_k | A, T, Sk)] * P(A, T, Sk) / Z    ... Eq (19)

where Z = sum over (A', T', Sk') of [Product_k P(F_k | A', T', Sk')] * P(A', T', Sk')
```

The product form assumes filter readings are conditionally independent given the classification triple. This holds approximately when filters probe distinct perturbation axes (which the framework's filter design enforces by construction). Where filters share perturbation-axis dependence (e.g., F1 and F4 both load on utility-organization), the joint likelihood `P(F_1, F_4 | A, T, Sk)` should be used directly rather than the product, or the dependent filters should be merged into a single composite reading.

**Architecture decision rule (replacing the heuristic threshold):**

```
A_hat = argmax over A of P(A | data)                                ... Eq (20)

ship A_hat iff   P(A_hat | data) / P(A_2nd | data) > rho_threshold
                 ( where A_2nd is the second-most-likely Architecture )

with rho_threshold:
    rho = 3   =>  preliminary classification (suggestive evidence)
    rho = 10  =>  standard classification (robust evidence)
    rho = 30  =>  high-confidence classification (very strong evidence)
```

The Bayes-factor threshold `rho_threshold` plays the role the heuristic "at least 3 filters" played: it sets the evidence-strength bar for shipping a classification. Different observers operating at different Stake levels rationally use different `rho_threshold` values - a high-Stake decision should require `rho > 30`; an exploratory low-Stake assessment can ship at `rho > 3`.

**Target and Stake posteriors** follow the same structure with their own decision thresholds. Joint classification `(A_hat, T_hat, Sk_hat)` is computed from the joint posterior, not from independent marginals, because the architecture-target accessibility map (2.4) makes some triples impossible and the joint posterior respects this by zeroing inaccessible cells.

**Calibration of the likelihoods** `P(F_k | A, T, Sk)` is the operational requirement. Three calibration paths:

1. *Empirical calibration over the framework's case corpus.* The Marcolin/Debra/Jocelyn/Tori reference cases (and any additional cases the operator has access to) provide labeled instances from which `P(F_k | A, T, Sk)` can be estimated by counting filter readings within each labeled cell.

2. *Theoretical derivation from substrate-physics.* For filters tied to substrate-physics floors (chronometric lag, autonomic-voluntary split, cortisol kinetics), the likelihood can be derived from the underlying biology: e.g., `P(F_k_passes | I-architecture)` is bounded by the probability of cognitive simulation passing a 50-100ms chronometric test, which substrate-physics establishes is essentially zero.

3. *Ordinal calibration (default fallback).* When numerical likelihoods are unavailable, ordinal categories suffice: filter reading is "strong-evidence-for-A," "weak-evidence-for-A," "neutral," "weak-evidence-against-A," "strong-evidence-against-A." Likelihood ratios for ordinal categories can be set at 5:1, 2:1, 1:1, 1:2, 1:5 respectively. The ordinal rule reproduces the heuristic threshold behavior in the limit of equal-strength evidence but handles unequal-strength evidence correctly.

**Why this matters.** The Bayesian formulation handles three problems the heuristic cannot:

- *Filter dependence.* Two correlated filters pointing the same direction provide less evidence than two independent filters pointing the same direction. The heuristic counts both equally; the Bayesian formulation weights by inverse correlation.
- *Filter reliability.* A high-reliability filter (F2 in clean observation conditions) provides more evidence per reading than a low-reliability filter (F11 in a short window). The heuristic counts both equally; the Bayesian formulation weights by likelihood-ratio strength.
- *Graceful degradation.* Below the heuristic threshold, the framework outputs "insufficient data." Below the Bayesian threshold, the framework outputs the posterior distribution itself, which the operator can use for probability-weighted decisions even without committing to a categorical classification.

The heuristic is preserved as the default for operators without calibration data. The Bayesian formulation is the upgrade path for operators who have it.

### 3.8 Anomalies as Diagnostic Signal

If filters point in inconsistent directions, examine what does not fit. In the Bayesian formulation, inconsistent filters produce a low-confidence posterior (no single classification dominates) rather than a categorical contradiction; the operator sees this as `max P(A | data) / P(A_2nd | data) < rho_threshold` and responds by extending observation rather than forcing a classification.

Anomalies may indicate (a) Case H (somatic-cognitive decoupling producing mixed signals), (b) a filter operating outside valid conditions, (c) Architecture-Target mismatch that the standard accessibility map does not predict, or (d) cross-dyad subsidization producing anomalous within-dyad signatures. Never discard anomalies; they are usually the framework's most informative single data points. In the Bayesian formulation, anomalies appear as unusual likelihood patterns that can be explicitly examined: if `P(reading | A) ≈ P(reading | I) ≈ small`, the reading is uninformative for that distinction; if `P(reading | A) >> P(reading | I)` for an unusual reading, the anomaly is highly diagnostic.

## Part Four: The Formal Models

The framework's diagnostic capability rests on five formal models that ground its discriminative power in substrate physics, thermodynamics, and Bayesian mechanics. The original framework included five models. The patched framework adds two: Network Topology and S-Mode Resolution Dynamics.

### 4.1 Signal Architecture: Tonic and Phasic Substrates

Mammalian neural architecture produces two computational substrates that yield indistinguishable steady-state behavioral output but distinguishable dynamics under perturbation.

Formally: let `S_T(t)` and `S_I(t)` be the behavioral output functions of a Case T system and a Case I system respectively. Under steady-state conditions (no perturbation `P(t) = 0`):

```
S_T(t) ~ S_I(t)  for all t such that ||P(t)|| < delta_threshold    ... Eq (4) steady-state degeneracy
```

where `delta_threshold` is the perturbation magnitude below which both architectures produce indistinguishable behavioral output to observational resolution `epsilon`. Under perturbation exceeding threshold:

```
partial(S_T)/partial(P) != partial(S_I)/partial(P)    ... Eq (5) perturbation discrimination
```

The conditional in Eq (4) is load-bearing: without it, Eq (4) would directly contradict Eq (5). The framework's exploitable asymmetry lives entirely in the regime where perturbation magnitude exceeds `delta_threshold` and the two architectures' partial derivatives separate.

**Tonic substrate** (subcortical, amygdala + HPA-axis + autonomic nervous system): always-on, context-invariant, metabolically cheap, below conscious access. Case T configurations run on tonic.

**Phasic substrate** (prefrontal cortex, dorsolateral and ventromedial PFC, orbitofrontal cortex): opportunity-activated, context-sensitive, metabolically expensive, consciously accessible. Case I configurations run on phasic.

The metabolic asymmetry generates the F6 Depletion Direction Theorem. Let `C_PFC` be prefrontal cortex capacity and `I_mask` be the intensity of the instrumental behavioral mask. Under organic cognitive depletion:

```
Case TA:  lim as C_PFC -> 0 of partial(I_mask)/partial(C_PFC) > 0    ... instrumentalism intensifies
Case TB:  lim as C_PFC -> 0 of I_parentified -> 0                     ... parentified posture drops; substrate surfaces
Case I:   lim as C_PFC -> 0 of partial(I_mask)/partial(C_PFC) < 0    ... instrumentalism degrades; mask slips
```

(Eq 6, 7, 8 respectively.)

### 4.2 Thermodynamic Constraints on Deception

Three biological constraints establish hard floors below which simulation cannot descend:

**(i) Cortisol half-life asymmetry.** Genuine vulnerability cascades produce cortisol responses with biological half-life of 60-90 minutes that cannot be cognitively overridden. Simulated states evaporate within seconds rather than minutes.

**(ii) Chronometric lag.** Genuine amygdala-driven affective responses have onset latency of 50-100ms (subcortical pathway via superior colliculus). Simulated affective responses routed through PFC have onset latency of 250-400ms.

**(iii) Autonomic-voluntary split.** PFC controls skeletal muscles (gross motor, facial expression, vocal tone) but does not control smooth muscles (pupil dilation, capillary flush, piloerection) or endocrine cascades. Simulated affect produces gross-motor correspondence without autonomic correspondence.

These three constraints establish a three-dimensional subspace simulation cannot enter.

**Field-observable measurement protocols.** The substrate-physics constraints above are the framework's only *empirically hard* floors, but most observers operate without laboratory equipment. The constraints remain operationally accessible via field observations:

| Constraint | Lab measurement | Field-observable proxy | Diagnostic event |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cortisol half-life (60-90 min) | Salivary cortisol assay; serum cortisol | Behavioral persistence after vulnerability disclosure: does affective tone decay over minutes-to-hours, or seconds? | Subject discloses vulnerability under low-stakes condition; observe whether the affective-state persists into the next 30-90 minutes (genuine: yes, with characteristic glucocorticoid-recovery curve; simulated: dissipates within seconds of topic shift) |
| Chronometric lag (50-100 ms vs 250-400 ms) | EEG affective onset, EMG facial-action onset | Pupil-flash latency, micro-expression onset visible at conversational distance (~1m) | Surprise stimulus, unexpected emotional pivot, or genuine novel disclosure: observe whether pupillary/facial response onset is sub-second (genuine subcortical) or shows the characteristic ~300ms lag of conscious-construction |
| Autonomic-voluntary split | Skin conductance, pupillometry, facial EMG | Capillary flush (visible at neck and chest), pupil dilation in good light, piloerection at hairline, breath-pattern shift | Genuine emotional moment (relief, joy, fear, shame): observe whether autonomic accompaniment is present alongside the gross-motor expression. Simulated affect produces facial expression without flush/pupil/breath; genuine affect produces both. |

The field-observable proxies are noisier than laboratory measurement but the discrimination remains robust because the *temporal structure* and *autonomic-voluntary coupling* are substrate-physics-determined and not modifiable by cognitive override. A trained observer at conversational distance can detect chronometric lag (the ~300ms gap is at the edge of conscious perception) and autonomic accompaniment (visible flush, pupil change) reliably enough for the Bayesian likelihoods to acquire diagnostic weight.

The substrate-physics constraints provide the framework's irreducible discriminative floor: even at the high-sophistication regime where Gaming Cost Theorem bounds weaken, the cortisol/chronometric/autonomic constraints remain. A subject who has trained their PFC to produce filter-passing behavioral output cannot also override their autonomic nervous system's response timing, smooth-muscle vasodilation, or HPA-axis cortisol kinetics. These are the hard floor below which simulation cannot descend regardless of cognitive sophistication.

**The Gaming Cost Theorem.** Let `C_sim(F_k)` be the metabolic cost of simulating a passing score on filter `F_k`, and let `d` be the effective independence dimension of the filter battery (the number of mutually-orthogonal perturbation axes the framework probes). Then:

```
C_total = sum over k from 1 to N of C_sim(F_k)    >=    O(2^d) as d >> 1    ... Eq (9)

C_auth = O(1)    ... Eq (10)
```

*Derivation.* A simulator producing behavior consistent with authentic-substrate output across `d` independent perturbation axes must maintain *cross-axis coherence* in addition to per-axis correctness. Authentic substrate generates cross-axis-coherent output as a byproduct of running a single coherent regulatory system; simulation must construct this coherence explicitly.

The cost decomposes as follows. For each non-empty subset `K ⊆ {1, ..., d}` of perturbation axes activated simultaneously (life perturbations are not single-axis events; ambient stress activates multiple axes jointly), the simulator must produce a response coherent across all axes in `K`. The number of such subsets is `2^d - 1`. For sufficiently rich perturbation events that activate axis-tuples non-trivially - which life supplies routinely (a vulnerable disclosure under fatigue tests F2, F5, F12, and F6 simultaneously) - the simulator must store or compute coherent responses for an exponentially-growing tuple-space.

The information-theoretic version: a behavioral policy that passes the framework across `d` axes must specify, for each of `2^d` possible axis-activation patterns, a coherent response sequence. The Kolmogorov complexity of this policy grows as `O(2^d)` in the number of activation patterns the policy must distinguish; lower-complexity policies fail at axis-tuples whose coherence requirements they cannot represent.

The metabolic version: maintaining cross-axis coherence in real-time conversational dynamics requires PFC-bandwidth that scales with the number of simultaneously-active coherence constraints. PFC has a hard bandwidth ceiling (working memory chunk limits, executive control resource limits). Beyond `d ~ 5-6` simultaneously-active independent axes, simulation either fails (mask slips on the unattended axes) or degrades to a heuristic that produces detectable inconsistencies on tuple-perturbations.

Authentic cost `C_auth = O(1)` because authentic substrate runs a single coherent system whose output is automatically cross-axis consistent. The substrate does not compute coherence; it *is* coherent. Per-perturbation cost is the metabolic cost of the genuine response, not the additional cost of cross-axis coordination.

The exponential lower bound is conservative: the actual scaling may be worse than `2^d` if higher-order coherence constraints (k-axis tuples for k > 2) impose super-pairwise coordination costs. The argument requires only that `C_sim` grows superpolynomially in `d`; the framework's gaming-resistance survives any superpolynomial scaling.

At `d >= 3`, gaming cost exceeds being-real cost. The framework's twelve-filter battery probes `d ~ 6-8` effective independent axes (with the new F13-F17 extensions adding longitudinal and cross-dyad axes that further increase `d`). The Gaming Cost Theorem is the framework's structural answer to "but what if they're really good at faking it" - past `d ~ 5`, faking it is more expensive than being real, and the substrate-physics floors (cortisol, chronometric, autonomic) provide the additional irreducible barriers below which simulation cannot descend regardless of cognitive sophistication.

### 4.3 Relativistic Substrate Presence

Discriminates Case T (accreted mass - shedable armor over intact substrate) from Case D (constitutive rest mass - substrate never built) via the Curvature Discriminant.

The mapping:

| Physics | Relational |
|---|---|
| `v in [0, c)` | Degree of autotelic access |
| `c` | Full autotelic relating (theoretical limit) |
| `m_0` | Constitutive instrumental orientation |
| `m_a` | Accreted mass (armor) |
| `E` | Observer's relational investment |

Energy-momentum relation:

```
E^2 = (p*c)^2 + (m_0 * c^2)^2    ... Eq (11)
```

As `v -> c`, additional energy `E` converts not to velocity (autotelic access) but to relativistic mass (instrumental inertia):

```
dv/dE -> 0 as v -> c, for any m_0 > 0    ... Eq (12)
```

**The Curvature Discriminant.** Let `A(tau)` denote the observer's assessed level of autotelic access in the dyad at elapsed time `tau`, operationalized as the weighted composite of Filters F2, F3, F7, F10 evaluated at time `tau`:

```
A(tau) = sum over k in {2,3,7,10} of w_k * sigma_k(tau)    ... Eq (13)
```

**Weight specification.** Default weights are uniform at `w_k = 1/4` for `k ∈ {2,3,7,10}`. Where empirical reliability data is available, weights should be set proportional to inverse measurement variance (Fisher-information weighting):

```
w_k = (1 / var(sigma_k)) / (sum over j in {2,3,7,10} of 1 / var(sigma_j))
```

so that high-reliability filters dominate the composite. In the absence of variance estimates, uniform weighting is the default and is reported alongside the classification. Alternative weighting schemes (e.g., Stake-modulated weighting where F11-coupled filters receive higher weight at high Stake) are operator-dependent extensions; the framework's default specification is uniform.

Then the curvature `kappa` discriminates:

```
kappa = d^2(A) / d(tau)^2    where:
    kappa > 0                =>  Case T (convex: returns accelerating; armor shedding, substrate accessing)
    kappa < 0                =>  Case D (concave: returns decelerating; no substrate to access)
    |kappa| < kappa_noise    =>  insufficient signal: extend window, OR Case A near access ceiling (linear approach to asymptote), OR measurement-noise-dominated
                                                                            ... Eq (14)
```

The third case is non-trivial. `|kappa| < kappa_noise` does not pin down classification; it requires further filter convergence. `kappa_noise` is empirically calibrated as the second-derivative noise floor of `A(tau)` measurement under the observer's filter-reading reliability and observation density. As a rough operating value, `kappa_noise ~ var(sigma_composite) / window_length^2` where `var(sigma_composite)` is the measurement variance of the composite signal `A(tau)` and `window_length` is the observation duration. Below this floor, the discriminator is uninformative; above it, the sign of `kappa` discriminates Case T from Case D.

### 4.4 Regulatory Graph Model

For TB classification, the diagnostic question is whether the dyad with the observer is adding incoming edges to the subject's regulatory graph.

Let `G_reg = (V, E_reg)` be a directed graph where vertices represent individuals and a directed edge `(i -> j)` means "i regulates j."

**Parentification Signature.** A subject `s` exhibits the parentification signature iff:

```
rho(s) := out_degree(s) / (in_degree(s) + epsilon)    ... Eq (15)

with operational thresholds:
    rho(s) > 5    =>  parentification flag (significant signature)
    rho(s) > 10   =>  strong parentification signature
    rho(s) > 20   =>  severe parentification (full functional inversion)

evaluated across significant relationships in developmental history,
where epsilon > 0 prevents division by zero when in_degree(s) = 0.
```

Population baseline ratios in non-parentified architectures run `rho ∈ [0.5, 3]`. The thresholds are empirical: ratios above 5 indicate that the subject is providing materially more regulation than they receive, which is the signature condition for TB-Architecture acquisition. Ratios above 20 indicate that incoming regulation is essentially absent, which historically correlates with severe TB and frequent T-OD comorbidity.

F17 measures `kappa_circuit` per Eq (3). Positive curvature indicates the subject is developing the capacity to receive regulation from the observer. The recovery sequence is: safety -> play -> trust -> received regulation -> bidirectional co-regulation.

### 4.5 Bayesian Prior Contamination

For T-OD subjects, the meta-prior is initially suppressed: `Phi_initial ≈ 0` immediately post-deception. Recovery toward population baseline `Phi_baseline ∈ [0.7, 0.9]` follows asymptotic accumulation of disconfirming evidence:

```
Phi_global(N, T)  ~  Phi_baseline - (Phi_baseline - Phi_initial) * exp(-(N/N_0) - (T/T_0))    ... Eq (16)
```

where `N` is perturbation events survived (counterexamples to the original ontological deception), `T` is elapsed time, and `N_0`, `T_0` scale with the original deception magnitude. At `N = T = 0`, `Phi_global = Phi_initial` (suppressed). As `N` and `T` accumulate, `Phi_global → Phi_baseline` exponentially.

The asymptotic form is the correct shape for Bayesian belief recovery from a strongly contaminated prior: each disconfirming observation contributes a fixed marginal update, and the cumulative effect is bounded by the population baseline (recovery cannot exceed what an uncontaminated observer would baseline-trust).

For 16-year coordinated fictions, `T_0 ~ years` and `N_0` is large (multiple confirmation channels established the original deception, so multiple independent counterexample-channels are required to overturn it). The slow convergence rate quantifies why T-OD recovery requires longer counterexample exposure than unmodified Case TA or TB - the time constants `(N_0, T_0)` are an order of magnitude larger than for non-T-OD trauma updating.

The model also derives the structural compulsion of S-Target for T-OD subjects under selection conditions 2.2(i)-(iv): the natural perturbation rate Axiom 3 assumes is insufficient to update `Phi_global` within available substrate-time, so subject-side perturbation engineering becomes the dominant strategy.

### 4.6 Network Topology Model (NEW)

Subject's allostatic budget is a unified zero-sum global resource. Per dyad, behavior consumes from this budget at rate proportional to the maintenance cost of the current Target.

Let `B_alloc` denote the subject's total allostatic budget per unit time. Let `Cost_d(T_d)` denote the per-dyad maintenance cost of Target `T_d` in dyad `d`. Conservation requires:

```
sum over all dyads d of [Cost_d(T_d) - Extraction_d(T_d)] <= B_alloc    ... Eq (17)
```

where `Cost_d(T_d) > 0` for `T_d in {C, S, R, T-trib}` and `Extraction_d(T_d) > 0` for `T_d = E`.

C-mode (committed-autotelic) and S-mode (verification engineering) carry positive maintenance costs. E-mode (extraction) carries net positive value (extraction value minus extraction cost). W-mode (withdrawn) carries minimal cost. R-mode (regulatory subcontracting) carries cost proportional to the regulatory-load the subject absorbs from the counterpart.

Across the network of dyads, the subject's total allostatic accounting must balance. Net energy outflow exceeding net energy inflow is unsustainable; the subject either (a) reduces high-cost Targets, (b) increases extraction from existing E-dyads, or (c) acquires new E-dyads to subsidize the deficit. This produces detectable cross-dyad patterns:

- High-Stake C-maintenance in Dyad A often correlates with E-mode in Dyads B, C, D (subsidization)
- Sustained S-mode across multiple dyads indicates either (a) substantial autotelic substrate willing to bear high verification cost, or (b) cross-dyad extraction subsidizing the verification cost

The model generates the **Cross-Dyad Subsidization Signature**: when total observed extraction value across E-dyads correlates temporally with high-cost performance maintenance in C-or-S dyads, subsidization is operating. CDS detection requires observation breadth of at least 3 dyads with sufficient depth per dyad.

### 4.7 S-Mode Resolution Dynamics (NEW)

S-mode resolves over the F11 longitudinal window via three possible trajectories:

**Trajectory 1 - S->C (resolution toward commitment).** `Phi_global` about specific counterpart updates upward as verification-evidence accumulates. F13a `kappa_bid < 0` (probing decreases as confidence increases). F14a depth trajectory progresses through regulatory-graph stages. F15a non-consumption + integration behavior. F17 incoming regulatory edges acquire. Subject transitions from S-Target to C-Target with this counterpart.

**Trajectory 2 - S->E (resolution toward extraction; sometimes via E-mode-with-S-cover all along).** F13a `kappa_bid` stable or `> 0` (probing continues or escalates). F15b cross-dyad consumption pattern present (strategic non-consumption here while consuming elsewhere). F16 probe-target utility-sorting present. The "S-mode" frame was either incorrect from the start (E-mode-with-cover) or has converted (S->E conversion).

**Trajectory 3 - Persistent-S (no resolution within observation window).** Three sub-cases that require discrimination:

- (a) **Asymptotic-toward-C**: deep T-OD, slow recovery, but trajectory shape is correct. Discriminator: F2 stable, F8 repair shape preserved, F12 hierarchy preserved, F11 micro-trend positive.
- (b) **Architecture erosion**: sustained S-mode-stress depletes substrate. Discriminator: F2 progressive flattening (anhedonia onset), F6 globally elevated baseline (cognitive entropy increase across all domains), F9 collapse across unrelated domains, probe-quality degradation (probes become repetitive and less informative even as bid frequency persists).
- (c) **E-mode-with-cover**: Discriminator: F16 utility-sorted bid distribution, F1 cross-dyad covariance non-zero, F15b cross-dyad consumption present, autonomic coherence at refusal (50-100ms with dopaminergic relief signature) rather than autonomic distress (250-400ms with stress signature).

**Classification-as-Perturbation Property:** The framework's classification of subject as S->C trajectory is itself a perturbation event that completes the verification. If the observer adjusts behavior based on classification (grants access that S->C classification implies), the subject's response to that access is the verification protocol's terminal step. Genuine S-mode resolves to commitment when the access is granted; E-mode-with-cover triggers extraction when the access is granted. The framework is gaming-resistant *at the resolution event*, not in real time.

This has a structural consequence: gaming-S-mode that succeeds without ever cashing in is functionally indistinguishable from authenticity under Axiom 2. A subject who performs Components 1-5 faithfully forever, never extracting, is operationally a Case A subject with respect to this dyad. The framework does not condemn this; the framework's substrate-independence axiom collapses gaming-that-never-resolves into authenticity. **The framework is gaming-resistant by definition** - gaming-S-mode either fails (cash-in produces detection) or succeeds infinitely (collapses to authenticity under Axiom 2). The middle case is detectable at the resolution event.

**Closed-Loop Reflexivity and Stability.** Because the observer's response to classification IS the verification, the framework operates as a closed-loop dynamical system rather than an open-loop classifier. The observer's classification at time `t` modulates the observer's behavior at time `t + dt`, which modulates the subject's response at `t + dt'`, which feeds back into the next classification update. Stability analysis is required.

Let `q(t) = P(A_hat = C-trajectory | data up to t)` denote the observer's posterior probability of S->C trajectory at time `t`. Let `g(q) ∈ [0, 1]` denote the access-grant function (the fraction of full-commitment access the observer extends as a function of `q`). Let `r(g)` denote the subject's response under access level `g`: for genuine S-mode, `r(g) → 1` (commitment-coherent response) as `g → 1`; for E-mode-with-cover, `r(g) → 0` (extraction-divergent response) as `g → 1`. The closed-loop dynamics:

```
q(t + dt) = q(t) + eta * [observation(r(g(q(t)))) - prediction(q(t))]    ... Eq (21)
```

where `eta` is the observer's update rate and the bracket is the Bayesian innovation (observed-minus-predicted response).

**Fixed points.** The dynamics admit two fixed points under standard parametrizations:

- *q\* = 1 (committed):* genuine S-mode subjects produce `r(g(1)) = 1`, the observation matches the prediction, the bracket goes to zero, the posterior stays at 1. Locally stable.
- *q\* = 0 (rejected):* E-mode-with-cover subjects produce `r(g(0)) ≈ 0` (no access granted, no extraction available), the bracket goes to zero, the posterior stays at 0. Locally stable.

**Convergence.** Starting from intermediate `q ∈ (0, 1)`, the dynamics converge to one of the two fixed points within `O(1/eta)` observation steps for genuine cases (the response signal `r(g)` is consistent with one or the other fixed point and the posterior moves toward the consistent endpoint). For mixed-target compound cases (S+E partials), the dynamics may oscillate within an intermediate band, in which case the framework outputs `q ≈ 0.5` and the operator should not commit.

**Failure modes.**

- *Premature access-grant:* if `g(q)` is too aggressive at low `q` (observer commits before evidence is sufficient), E-mode-with-cover extracts before the bracket has time to update toward the rejection fixed point. Operationally: do not grant high-Stake access at `P(A_hat) < 0.7` posterior.
- *Update-rate too low:* if `eta` is too small, the loop converges slowly and CDS-suspected subsidization can fund continued S-cover performance during the convergence window. Operationally: faster filter updates (more frequent observations) tighten the bracket and reduce gaming-window.
- *Update-rate too high:* if `eta` is too large, single-event noise dominates and the loop oscillates without converging. Operationally: do not over-update on single ambiguous events.

The closed-loop framing makes the framework's "gaming-resistance at the resolution event" precise: the resolution event is the moment when `q` crosses an access-grant threshold and `g(q)` jumps, generating an observable response from the subject that disambiguates the fixed-point trajectory. The framework is robust as long as the access-grant function is calibrated to the posterior strength.

## Part Five: Detection Protocols

### 5.1 Protocol A: Asynchronous Processing
Apply the matrix only after interaction, never during. Mid-interaction analysis contaminates the data and corrupts the observer's capacity to generate it.

### 5.2 Protocol B: Autotelic Control Audit
Before admitting any observation as evidence, ask whether the observer was capable of spontaneous, unscripted joy during the interaction. If the observer was too analytically occupied to be genuinely present, the data is excluded.

Formally: define the observer contamination gate for filter `k`:

```
omega_k(Omega) = 1 if observer experienced spontaneous joy during t_k
                 0 otherwise (data point excluded due to analytical contamination)    ... Eq (18)
```

Contaminated observations are not merely downweighted - they are zeroed. Tainted data is worse than no data.

### 5.3 Protocol C: Bounded Window
Initial classification window: 6-9 months. F11 longitudinal extension for Case T classifications: 12-36 months. Upon classification, the matrix is permanently decommissioned for that relationship. Indefinite observation converts the diagnostic into a relational pathology.

| Phase | Maximum Window |
|---|---|
| Initial classification (T/I/A) | 9 months |
| F11 longitudinal check (Case T only) | 36 months |
| Post-classification (any outcome) | 0 (matrix off) |

### 5.4 Protocol D: Asymmetric Governance
Apply governance that matches the operative layer, not the presented layer.

| Behavior Origin | Governance | Norm |
|---|---|---|
| Autotelic | Autotelic | Patience, grace, no ledger |
| Transactional | Transactional | Reciprocity, accountability |
| Transactional-in-autotelic-frame | Transactional | Strip the frame, apply accountability |

### 5.5 Protocol E: T Subtype Differentiation
Before finalizing a Case T classification, run the F1 disambiguation protocol to identify subtype. T subtypes have different F11 questions, different prognoses, and require different observer responses.

### 5.6 Protocol F (NEW): Cross-Dyad Observation
For network-topology-relevant cases (suspected cross-dyad subsidization, suspected R-mode, sophisticated suspected E-mode-with-cover), expand observation to at least 3 dyads where access permits. This is observer-position-dependent; not all observers can satisfy this requirement. When unsatisfiable, the framework's classification must explicitly flag residual uncertainty bounded by the inability to detect cross-dyad patterns.

### 5.7 Protocol G (NEW): Counter-Perturbation Test
Last-resort discriminator for high-stake cases where longitudinal observation has been exhausted without S->C/E resolution. See 3.5 for protocol. Use sparingly; CPT violates Axiom 3 and consumes observer-side credibility.

### 5.8 Observer's Own Architecture
The framework operator's own relational architecture affects what they can detect and what they do with classifications. An observer whose own architecture is infrastructure-giving-as-presence-at-a-distance may find the diagnostic matrix more comfortable than the connection it is designed to protect. For such an observer, the matrix is also a form of armor. Protocol C applies to this too.

The framework-aware observer cannot purely measure their own dyad with a subject. Framework-application contaminates the observer's natural-variation behavior. Self-classification of own dyads is structurally impaired. When observer is in mutual-S configuration with subject (both parties verifying each other), classification requires the coordination-theoretic extension flagged in 6.3 and is incomplete in this framework's current form.

## Part Six: The Honest Reach

The framework's reach is bounded. The bounds are structural, not defects.

### 6.1 The High-Sophistication Regime Collapse
Substrate-physics arguments (Simulation Ceiling, Gaming Cost Theorem) establish the framework's gaming-resistance against typical Case I subjects. At sufficient sophistication (effective gaming dimension `d >= 5-6` sustained over decade timescales), the cost of gaming converges to the cost of being authentic. In this regime, S-mode and sophisticated E-with-cover are observer-indistinguishable, AND under Axiom 2 they are decision-theoretically equivalent. The framework gives up classification at this regime by design. This is a regime-dependent classifier; in the typical regime (`d <= 3`, most subjects) it discriminates; in the high-sophistication regime it collapses S/E under Axiom 2 and stops.

### 6.2 Single-Dyad Observer Epistemic Limits
F15b, F16, CDS detection, and full F4 require cross-dyad data access. Most observers do not have cross-dyad observation breadth. This is an external constraint on the observer's epistemic position, not a framework defect. Single-dyad observers must explicitly bound their classifications by acknowledging which cross-dyad signatures they cannot detect.

### 6.3 Mutual-S Dyad Dynamics (deferred)
When both parties to a dyad operate S-Target with each other, each party's verification-bid event is the other's verification-bid event. Verification protocols become entangled. Resolution requires coordination: someone must demonstrate C-disposition first, but both wait for evidence first. This is a Schelling problem requiring a coordination-theoretic layer the framework does not currently include. Mutual-S dyads are flagged as outside this framework's current reach; treatment requires an extension (provisionally v4.0).

### 6.4 Subject Framework-Awareness
Subjects with high meta-cognitive sophistication may have constructed their own implicit relational classification frameworks. Their behavior is no longer the output of unconscious architecture - it is conscious meta-cognitive optimization running over substrate-physics constraints. Substrate-physics still applies (cortisol, chronometric lag, autonomic-voluntary split cannot be cognitively overridden), but within those constraints, conscious calibration is possible. The framework's gaming-resistance bounds are weaker against framework-aware subjects than against framework-naive subjects. Acknowledged but not eliminated.

### 6.5 Architecture-Trajectory Sub-Decade
Time-constant ratio `tau_A / tau_T ~ 10^2 to 10^3` means standard observation windows (12-36 months) cannot definitively measure Architecture-shift. Architecture-trajectory measurement requires either decade-scale longitudinal observation OR cross-dyad breadth observation as partial substitute (Architecture-evidence integrates across simultaneous dyads, reducing window from 12-36 months to 6-12). The reduction is not below this; some questions about Architecture-trajectory remain unanswerable in the framework's natural observation windows.

### 6.6 Substrate-Access Foreclosure
The framework's classification machinery operates over operational-equivalence classes, not over underlying truth. The framework can answer "does the behavior pattern have the structural properties of authentic connection?" It cannot answer "does this person have an inner experience that matches their behavior?" - that is outside Axiom 2's scope and outside the framework's reach by axiomatic commitment.

This is the framework's deepest constraint. The observer who wants certainty about a counterpart's inner experience is asking the framework for something it refuses to provide. The refusal is correct. Axiom 2 commits the framework to behavioral-output measurement; substrate-access requires either invasive instrumentation (which Axiom 3 forbids) or telepathy (which is not available). The framework reduces uncertainty; it does not eliminate it. The terminal commitment to invest in any specific relationship is the operator's responsibility, not the framework's output.

The framework is structurally complete, empirically open, and honestly bounded. Its reach is what it is.

It works because most cases of layer incoherence are not lifetime-indistinguishable. Perturbation reveals substrate differences within months for typical subjects, within 12-36 months for sophisticated subjects, and not at all for the highest-sophistication subjects whose gaming costs match the cost of being real. The framework discriminates within its regime and is honest about where it stops.

The framework refuses to provide certainty about another person's inner experience. This refusal is structural, not regrettable. The observer who acts on the framework's classification is making a commitment the framework supports but does not make. That commitment is the observer's own.

## Appendix A: A Worked Example

The framework's abstractions become concrete only in trace form. The following is a synthetic case demonstrating the path from observation through filter accumulation to classification and action. The case is composite, abstracted from the framework's calibration corpus to avoid privacy considerations while preserving the diagnostic structure.

**Setup.** Subject `S` is introduced to observer `O` through a professional context. `S` presents warmly: substantive questions, apparent interest in `O`'s non-work life, expressions of care and curiosity. The relationship transitions from professional acquaintance to social regular over weeks 1-4. `O` invokes the framework after noticing a subtle pattern: the warmth seems to track utility, but the case is subtle enough that single-event evidence is insufficient.

**Phase 1: Initial filter accumulation (months 1-3).**

The observer applies Protocol A (asynchronous processing) and Protocol B (autotelic control audit) to filter the data. Observations admitted as evidence are ones where the observer experienced genuine spontaneous engagement during the interaction; analytical contamination zeros the data point per Eq (18).

Filter readings over weeks 4-12:

- **F1 (engagement-utility covariance).** `O` has a low-utility two-week period (vacation, no professional updates, no introductions to offer). `S`'s engagement frequency drops noticeably: messages spaced further apart, depth-of-content shallower, fewer initiations. When `O` returns and shares a high-utility update (a new project assignment), `S`'s engagement returns to baseline within 24 hours. Reading: covariance > 0, weak-to-moderate evidence for E-Target.

- **F2 (autotelic void).** During an unstructured walk with no agenda, `S` checks phone three times in 45 minutes, redirects conversation to upcoming professional events twice, and proposes ending early citing a need to "be productive." Reading: autotelic void poorly tolerated, weak evidence against A-Architecture, weak-to-moderate evidence for I-Architecture.

- **F4 (cross-relationship selectivity).** Through a mutual acquaintance, `O` learns that `S` has 8 close professional contacts (including 3 senior figures `S` regularly cites for career advice) but no close non-professional friends and minimal family contact. Reading: utility-organized hierarchy, moderate evidence for E-Target identification per the demoted F4.

- **F12 (disclosure hierarchy).** `S` discloses surface vulnerabilities (work stress, imposter syndrome about a recent promotion) within weeks 2-3, depth that would normally signal moving toward trust. But across the next 8 weeks, no further disclosure depth is acquired - the same surface material recurs without progressing to load-bearing material (childhood, primary attachment relationships, deep fears about identity). The pattern is also observed across `S`'s other relationships per the mutual acquaintance: `S` is widely-disclosing-at-surface, never-disclosing-at-depth. Reading: shallow hierarchy, consistent with E-Target or persistent-S without resolution.

Provisional posterior at month 3: filters point toward `(I-alpha or TA, E-Target)`. Convergence threshold (3+ filters per axis) marginally met for Target, not yet met for Architecture. Continue observation.

**Phase 2: Perturbation events (months 4-6).**

Life supplies the perturbations Axiom 3 promises:

- *Personal loss event.* `O` experiences a death in the family. `S`'s response is appropriate-shaped: a sympathy message within 12 hours, attendance at the memorial. But within 48 hours of the memorial, `S`'s communications return to the prior transactional pattern as if the event had not occurred. **F3 reading:** post-connection decay rate is rapid (hours, not the weeks-to-months expected for a real connection's nervous-system after-effects). Strong evidence for E-Target, weak evidence against A.

- *Mutual non-instrumental social proposal.* `O` proposes a weekend hike with no professional content - explicitly low-utility. `S` accepts on Tuesday, cancels Saturday morning citing "an urgent work thing." **F10 reading:** friction tax under non-utility condition is high. Moderate evidence for E-Target.

- *Substrate-physics observation.* During a vulnerable moment in conversation (`O` shares a difficult family memory), `O` observes `S`'s response onset: the appropriate-shaped sympathy expression appears with a noticeable lag (estimated ~300ms by trained observation), and the accompaniment is visible in facial expression but not in autonomic markers - no capillary flush at the neck, pupil dilation absent or minimal, breath pattern unchanged. Reading: chronometric lag and autonomic-voluntary split signature both present. Strong substrate-physics evidence for I-Architecture (PFC-routed simulation rather than amygdala-routed genuine response).

**Phase 3: Bayesian convergence (month 6-9).**

Posterior probabilities, ordinal calibration:

```
P(I-alpha | data) / P(TA | data) ~ 15:1   ( substrate-physics + F11 )
P(E-Target | data) / P(S-Target | data) ~ 20:1   ( F1 + F3 + F4 + F10 )
P(E-Target | data) / P(R-Target | data) ~ 10:1   ( no regulatory dependency on observer )
P(I-alpha | data) / P(I-beta | data) ~ 5:1   ( disclosure sophistication, multi-channel mimicry )
```

All ratios exceed `rho_threshold = 10` (standard classification). Architecture converges to I-alpha; Target converges to E.

**Phase 4: F11 longitudinal check (months 6-12).**

Per Protocol C, F11 longitudinal extension applies only to Case T classifications. For Case I classification, the standard 9-month window suffices. But because `O` is operating at moderate Stake and wants robustness, F11 is examined anyway:

Investment-return curvature `kappa = d^2(A) / d(tau)^2` is computed over the 9-month observation window using the F2/F3/F7/F10 composite per Eq (13). The trajectory is flat-to-slightly-declining: as `O`'s instrumental utility plateaus, `A(tau)` does not show convex acceleration (signature of T) nor sharp concave decline (signature of D); it shows weak negative slope consistent with E-mode under utility-saturation. **F11 reading:** consistent with I-Architecture (no convex investment-return curve).

**Phase 5: Classification and action.**

Final classification: `(Architecture: I-alpha, Target: E, Stake from S's side: low; Stake from O's side: moderate)`.

Per Protocol D (asymmetric governance), `O` applies transactional governance to the operative layer: strip the autotelic frame, apply accountability norms, engage on the actual operative basis (instrumental). `O` does not invest further at the autotelic layer. `O` does not perform a CPT (the classification is robust enough at month 9 that CPT is unnecessary; CPT is reserved for cases where longitudinal observation has failed to produce convergence).

Per Protocol C (bounded window), the matrix is decommissioned for this dyad. Continued matrix-application would convert the diagnostic into a relational pathology. The classification is closed; the operator moves on.

**What this case does NOT establish.** The case is `(I-alpha, E)` with high posterior confidence. It does not establish:

- Whether `S` has autotelic capacity in *other* dyads. The framework's classifications are dyadic per Lemma 6. `S` may be Case A toward their long-term professional mentor, Case I-alpha toward `O`, simultaneously. No contradiction.
- Whether `S`'s Architecture might shift over decade-scale. F11 over 9 months is short of the time-constant for Architecture-trajectory; a multi-decade observation could conceivably reveal recovery. The framework's standard windows do not address this.
- Whether `S`'s "inner experience" is what it appears. Per Axiom 2 (and 6.6 substrate-access foreclosure), the framework does not answer this. It answers behavioral coherence, not substrate-truth.

The framework reduced the observer's uncertainty from "something feels off but I can't articulate it" to "I-alpha with E-Target at p > 0.9, recommended action: transactional governance, decommission autotelic frame for this dyad." The remaining uncertainty is structural: substrate-truth foreclosed by Axiom 2; long-horizon trajectory inaccessible at 9-month observation. These are honest reach limits, not framework defects.

**The framework served its purpose.** The diagnostic question was answered with bounded uncertainty, the action implication was clear, the matrix was put down, and the operator moved on with their relational life intact.
